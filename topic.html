1. Topological Groups

A topological group $G$ is defined as a group equipped with a topological space structure such that the group multiplication $G \times G \to G$ and the inversion map $G \to G$ are continuous.

ğŸ’¡ Definition: Lie Group If $G$ further admits the structure of a smooth manifold such that both multiplication and inversion are smooth, then $G$ is referred to as a Lie group.

For an element $x$ in a topological space $X$, we denote the orbit of $x$ as $\text{Orb}(x)$, which represents the set of images of $x$ under various translations.
